Team 31: Project Looking Mass - Detecting and Analyzing Gravitational Lensing

What is Graviational Lensing?

General relativity describes the effects of gravity as the curvature of spacetime. One of the consequences of the theory is that light can bend around mass, producing a lens-like effect -- this is called gravitational lensing.

What's it look like?

This effect is significant and visible around massive objects, such as galaxies, clumps of dark matter, and black holes. The resulting image is often distorted, and sometimes a galaxy may form two or more images of itself.

Lensing In Practice


Why Detect It?

Detecting gravitational lensing helps to detect massive objects that may otherwise be undetectable, and the intensity of the lensing tells about the distribution and amount of mass. Studying gravitational lensing is a way to advance our knowledge of black holes and dark matter, both of which are hot topics in physics and cosmology.

What's Our Goal?

This team aims to create an app, Looking Mass, that is able to artificially simulate gravitational lensing in images using image processing. The app will have a user friendly interface that will be easy to navigate and include a tutorial for its features.

As a secondary goal (if the above goals are met), the team would also like to add a component to Looking Mass that uses a convolutional neural network (CNN) that can be used to detect gravitational lensing in images. Such a tool may be useful for scientists and researchers as manual methods of detecting gravitational lensing can be time consuming.
